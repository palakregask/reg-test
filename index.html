Environmental Protection and Emissions Regulations — REG/2020-45

Section 12 — Emission Reporting Obligations

Current to 2025-11-01 · Last amended 2025-08-15 · Previous versions

Part 5, Division 2 — Annual Reporting Requirements

Note: This section applies to all licensed operators of Class A and Class B industrial facilities as defined under section 4 of these Regulations. See also: Schedule 1 — List of Controlled Substances.

Obligation to Report

12 Every operator of a licensed facility must submit an annual emissions report to the Minister no later than March 31 of each year, covering the preceding calendar year.
(2) The report referred to in subsection (1) must include
(a) the total volume of each regulated substance released into the atmosphere, expressed in tonnes;
(b) the monitoring methodology used and the name of any accredited third-party verifier;
(c) a comparison of reported emissions against the facility's permitted thresholds under Schedule 4; and
(d) any corrective actions taken in response to exceedances identified in the previous reporting period.
(3) An operator who fails to submit the report within the time prescribed by subsection (1) is liable to an administrative penalty as set out in Schedule 4.

Format and Submission

13 Reports must be submitted electronically using the National Emissions Registry portal in the format prescribed by the Minister.
(2) Where technical difficulties prevent electronic submission, an operator may submit a paper report accompanied by a written explanation. The Minister may, at their discretion, accept or reject such a submission.

Public Disclosure

14.2 The Minister must publish a summary of all annual emissions reports received under section 12(1) on the National Regulatory Portal no later than September 30 of each year, in a format accessible to the general public.
(2) The published summary must include, for each facility,
(a) the name and location of the facility;
(b) total reported emissions by substance and class; and
(c) whether the facility met, exceeded, or fell below its permitted threshold for the reporting period.
(3) An operator may apply to the Minister to redact commercially sensitive information from the public summary. The Minister must render a decision within 45 days and may grant a redaction only where disclosure would cause demonstrable competitive harm that outweighs the public interest in transparency.
14 No operator may dismiss, suspend, demote, or otherwise penalise an employee who, in good faith, reports a suspected violation of these Regulations to the Minister or to an inspector.
(2) An employee who believes they have been penalised in contravention of subsection (1) may file a complaint with the Labour Relations Board within 60 days of the alleged reprisal.
(3) Upon receipt of a complaint under subsection (2), the burden of proof lies with the operator to demonstrate that any adverse action taken against the employee was unrelated to the report made under subsection (1).
