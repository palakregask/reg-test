Environmental Protection and Emissions Regulations — REG/2020-45

Section 12 — Emission Reporting Obligations

Current to 2025-11-01 · Last amended 2025-08-15 · Previous versions

Part 5, Division 2 — Annual Reporting Requirements

Note: This section applies to all licensed operators of Class A and Class B industrial facilities as defined under section 4 of these Regulations.

Definitions

11.1 In this Division,
(a) "regulated substance" means any greenhouse gas or toxic compound listed in Schedule 1, including carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide, and hydrofluorocarbons;
(b) "reporting period" means the calendar year immediately preceding the date on which a report is due;
(c) "senior officer" means a director, chief executive officer, or any person designated in writing by the board of directors to act in that capacity for the purposes of these Regulations.

Obligation to Report

12 Every operator of a licensed facility must submit an annual emissions report to the Minister no later than June 30 of each year, covering the preceding calendar year.
(2) The report referred to in subsection (1) must include
(a) the total volume of each regulated substance released into the atmosphere, expressed in tonnes;
(b) the monitoring methodology used and the name of any accredited third-party verifier;
(c) a comparison of reported emissions against the facility's permitted thresholds under Schedule 4; and
(d) any corrective actions taken in response to exceedances identified in the previous reporting period.
(3) An operator who fails to submit the report within the time prescribed by subsection (1) is liable to an administrative penalty in accordance with the following schedule:
Days Late Penalty (Class A Facility) Penalty (Class B Facility)
1–15 days$5,000$2,000
16–30 days$15,000$6,000
31+ days$30,000$12,000

Format and Submission

13 Reports must be submitted electronically using the National Emissions Registry portal in the format prescribed by the Minister.
(2) Where technical difficulties prevent electronic submission, an operator may submit a paper report accompanied by a written explanation. The Minister may, at their discretion, accept or reject such a submission.

Data Retention and Record Keeping

12.1 Every operator must retain all records, data, and supporting documentation used in the preparation of an annual emissions report for a minimum period of seven years from the date of submission.
(2) Records referred to in subsection (1) must include
(a) raw monitoring data collected from all continuous emissions monitoring systems;
(b) calibration logs and maintenance records for all monitoring equipment;
(c) all correspondence with the accredited third-party verifier; and
(d) internal audit reports and any corrective action plans prepared during the reporting period.
(3) Records must be made available to an inspector within 48 hours of a written request. Failure to produce records within this period constitutes a separate offence under section 130 independent of any underlying reporting violation.

Third-Party Verification

14.1 Every operator whose facility emits more than 10,000 tonnes of regulated substances annually must engage an accredited independent verifier to audit and certify the emissions report before submission to the Minister.
(2) The independent verifier must hold current accreditation under the National Environmental Auditors Standards Order and must not have a financial interest in the facility being audited.
(3) A verification report signed by the accredited verifier must be appended to the annual emissions report as a Schedule A attachment. Failure to include a verification report renders the submission incomplete for the purposes of section 12(1).

Certification

14 Every report must be certified by a senior officer of the operator as being accurate and complete to the best of their knowledge.
(2) A person who certifies a report knowing it to be false or misleading commits an offence under section 130 of these Regulations.

Part 5, Division 3 — Emissions Offsets and Trading

Offset Credits

15 An operator whose reported emissions exceed the permitted threshold under Schedule 4 may apply to the Minister for an offset credit allocation in lieu of paying an administrative penalty, subject to the conditions set out in this Division.
(2) To qualify for offset credits, an operator must demonstrate that
(a) the exceedance was caused by circumstances beyond the operator's reasonable control, including equipment failure, natural disaster, or supply chain disruption;
(b) the operator has an approved emissions reduction plan filed with the Minister within 30 days of the exceedance; and
(c) the operator has not been granted offset credits in respect of the same facility in the preceding two reporting periods.

Trading Between Facilities

16 An operator who achieves verified emissions below their permitted threshold may register the surplus as tradeable credits on the National Emissions Registry in accordance with the Emissions Trading Framework Order.
(2) Credits registered under subsection (1) may be transferred to another licensed operator to offset that operator's exceedance, provided that
(a) both facilities are licensed under the same commodity class;
(b) the transfer is recorded on the National Emissions Registry within 14 days of the agreement; and
(c) the total offset applied does not exceed 20% of the receiving facility's annual permitted threshold.
(3) Credits expire five years from the date of registration and may not be carried forward, transferred posthumously, or applied retroactively to a prior reporting period.

Revocation of Credits

17 The Minister may revoke any offset credit where it is determined that the underlying emissions data was inaccurate, falsified, or obtained through a methodology not approved under these Regulations. Revocation does not relieve the operator of liability for the original exceedance.
